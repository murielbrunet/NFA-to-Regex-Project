Practice Problem 2



First, we will look at just a. This is the NFA for regex: a.


Then we will look at just b. This is the NFA for regex: b.


Then we will concatenate a and b together, by adding an ε arc.


For the union of two expressions, we need ε arcs. The union symbol is similar to boolean or.


Lastly, the Kleene star means to recurse the regex inside the parentheses of the star, so we add ε arcs to go back to the beginning of the NFA.
